ABOUT US 


It is not lost on us that the class of 2020 has had a particularly deep commitment to and relationship with social issues. Our freshman year, we protested in mass numbers after the election of Donald Trump. Our sophomore year, we walked out and mobilized behind the fight for gun control. Our junior year, we organized several demonstrations in support of our teachers to pressure our district to pay our educators adequate wages before the strike. And now, our senior year has been cut short by a pandemic that disproportionately affects communities of color and our graduation punctuated by the largest civil rights demonstrations in recent history. We wanted to create these class shirts to both commemorate the class of 2020 and raise funds to support the ongoing efforts against police brutality and systemic racism in our justice system. All proceeds will go to the Anti Police-Terror Project and the People’s Breakfast Oakland. You can find information about these organizations below.

Anti-Police Terror Project 

The Anti Police-Terror Project is a Black-led, multi-racial, intergenerational coalition that seeks to build a replicable and sustainable model to eradicate police terror in communities of color. They support families surviving police terror in their fight for justice, documenting police abuses and connecting impacted families and community members with resources, legal referrals, and opportunities for healing.

People's Breakfast Oakland 

The People’s Breakfast Oakland (PBO) is a Black socialist grassroots group that has been serving hot breakfasts to homeless communities in West Oakland since 2017. They have set up a bail fund for protestors in Oakland, and have been working to bring home anyone arrested for protesting against police brutality in recent weeks. All donations are being directed to their bail fund as of now, and any leftover funds will go to their continuing work to feed homeless communities.
